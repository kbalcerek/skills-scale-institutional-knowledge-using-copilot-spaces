# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape the end-to-end user experience so solutions are intuitive, accessible, and aligned to customer needs.

### Responsibilities
- Run or support discovery activities (interviews, journey mapping, usability testing)
- Translate user needs into flows, wireframes, and interaction guidance
- Partner with Product Managers to define user-centered acceptance criteria
- Collaborate with Developers to ensure implementation fidelity and accessibility
- Validate design outcomes with customer feedback and usage signals

### Typical Communication
- Regular design reviews with Product and Engineering
- Shared prototypes and design annotations in planning and implementation
- Usability findings and recommendations shared with PM/PdM and stakeholders

### Value to Project Outcomes
- Reduces rework by identifying usability gaps early
- Improves adoption and customer satisfaction by making workflows easier to use
- Strengthens quality by embedding accessibility and consistency in delivery

---

## DevOps / SRE

### Role Summary
DevOps/SRE engineers enable reliable, secure, and observable delivery pipelines and production operations.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Establish environment readiness, monitoring, alerting, and SLO/SLA guardrails
- Partner with Developers on operability, rollback, and incident response readiness
- Support capacity planning, performance baselines, and resilience testing
- Guide release risk decisions with production reliability insights

### Typical Communication
- Environment and release readiness checkpoints with PM, Developers, and QA
- Operational risk updates in weekly delivery syncs
- Incident channels, post-incident reviews, and reliability action tracking

### Value to Project Outcomes
- Shortens lead time to production with safer, repeatable deployments
- Reduces downtime and customer impact through proactive observability
- Improves confidence in releases via clear rollback and response plans

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts convert business goals into clear, testable requirements and help align stakeholders on scope and expected outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and refine business requirements and process impacts
- Map current-state and future-state workflows with assumptions and constraints
- Support backlog refinement with clear acceptance criteria and dependencies
- Maintain traceability between business objectives, requirements, and deliverables
- Surface policy/compliance considerations early in planning

### Typical Communication
- Requirement workshops with stakeholders, Product, and Project Managers
- Backlog clarification sessions with Developers and QA
- Decision logs and requirement updates shared in project channels

### Value to Project Outcomes
- Reduces ambiguity and handoff delays by clarifying requirements early
- Improves delivery predictability with better dependency and scope definition
- Helps teams deliver measurable business outcomes, not just completed tasks

---

## Customer Support / Success

### Role Summary
Customer Support/Success teams represent customer reality in delivery and ensure launch readiness for adoption, enablement, and issue response.

### Responsibilities
- Provide recurring customer pain points, usage trends, and support insights
- Review release readiness for customer-facing changes and known issues
- Prepare enablement artifacts (FAQs, troubleshooting notes, support scripts)
- Coordinate launch communications and early-life support plans
- Feed post-release customer outcomes back into prioritization and retrospectives

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release readiness and known-issue reviews with PM/PdM/Engineering
- Ongoing issue trend reporting and escalation paths after launch
- Customer feedback summaries for roadmap and retrospective discussions

### Value to Project Outcomes
- Improves adoption by aligning releases with real customer needs
- Reduces support burden through better preparedness and documentation
- Accelerates learning loops between production behavior and future planning
